Primary care, diagnostics, and specialty referrals are coordinated through one chart so your story is not repeated room to room.

Care teams use shared records and structured handoffs so follow-up plans stay clear after every visit.

Chronic-condition programs, medication reviews, and preventive screenings are scheduled on a timeline that fits your goals—not a one-size-fits-all checklist.

When you need a specialist, we help coordinate timing, share records securely, and loop results back to your primary team so nothing falls between the cracks.

  • Same-day acute visits when capacity allows
  • Secure messaging for non-urgent questions
  • After-visit summaries in plain language
  • Video and phone visits when appropriate for follow-up and medication checks
  • Care navigation for imaging, labs, and referrals with clear prep instructions
  • Evening and weekend options for select services—ask when you book

We work with many major insurance plans. Please bring your member ID card and a photo ID to every visit so we can verify coverage at check-in.

For planned or elective procedures, we can often provide a good-faith estimate of expected costs—including your deductible, copay, or coinsurance—before you schedule.

If your plan requires an authorization or referral from a primary care physician, arrange that before specialty appointments to avoid delays or unexpected charges.

HSA, FSA, and HRA accounts can often be used for eligible copays, deductibles, and qualified expenses—keep your card handy and save receipts as your plan requires.

Claims are filed electronically in most cases; if your insurer requests additional information, responding promptly helps avoid balance surprises on your statement.

  • Out-of-network benefits may still apply; ask us to review your plan details with you.
  • Flexible payment options may be available for balances after insurance processing.
  • Price estimates are based on information available at the time of request; final amounts depend on how your claim is processed.
  • Workers’ compensation and auto-injury visits need correct claim or case details at check-in—bring any paperwork you have.

Coverage and benefits vary by plan. For the most accurate information, confirm specifics with your carrier or employer benefits office.

Quick answers to topics patients ask about most. For anything urgent, call your care team or seek emergency care as appropriate.

How do I reschedule?

Call the number on your appointment confirmation or use the patient portal link in your email. Giving at least 24 hours’ notice helps us offer the time to someone else who needs care.

What should I bring to my first visit?

Bring your insurance card, photo ID, a list of current medications and doses, and any recent test results or records from other providers you’d like us to review.

How do refills and prescriptions work?

Refill requests are reviewed during business hours. Allow a few days for processing; controlled substances may require an in-person or telehealth visit per policy and law.

How does billing work after my visit?

We submit claims to your insurer first. You may receive an explanation of benefits (EOB) from them, then a statement from us for any patient responsibility. Questions? Contact our billing office using the number on your statement.

Are telehealth visits available?

Many follow-ups and consults can be done by video or phone when clinically appropriate. Ask when you schedule—we’ll confirm whether your visit type and insurance qualify.
